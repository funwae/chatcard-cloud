What is ChatCard?

ChatCard is a digital identity card for your AI presence. It doesn’t log you into sites—it tells AI systems who you are, how you like to interact, and what they’re allowed to do on your behalf.

A ChatCard can carry:

  • Your AI persona – language, tone, formality, accessibility preferences.
  • Your model choices – preferred providers or models, where supported.
  • Your permissions – which actions AIs can take for you on a given site.
  • – how your data can be logged, stored, or used for training.

When a site supports ChatCard, your AI doesn’t start from scratch. It meets you as you.

For people who use AI everywhere

Today, every new AI app asks you to re-teach it who you are. ChatCard is a simple way to carry a consistent AI presence with you instead.

One persona, many places

Set your preferred language, tone, and style once. Sites that support ChatCard can honor those preferences without you reconfiguring every time.

Keep your AI on your side

A ChatCard tells AI what it’s allowed to do: read pages, fill forms, call actions, or just summarize. You decide the scope per site.

Stay in control

You can see which sites you’ve connected, what permissions they have, and revoke them with a click. Your card is yours—not any one company’s lock-in.

Bring your history

Over time, ChatCard can link to trails of your past AI-assisted work (stored where you choose), so new apps can help you pick up where you left off.

For developers and platforms

ChatCard gives you a standard way to welcome “bring-your-own-AI” users without inventing your own identity protocol.

Offer a better first-run experience

When a new user lands with a ChatCard, you already know their language, tone, and accessibility preferences. You can personalize safely from the first interaction.

Standardized AI permissions

Use ChatCard scopes to define what user AIs can do on your site: read-only, suggest-only, or act-with-confirmation. No bespoke auth dance required.

AI-enabled content, consistently

Combine ChatCard with “AI-enabled content” metadata to expose structure and actions in a machine-readable way. Any compatible AI can understand your pages without brittle scraping.

Provider-neutral

ChatCard doesn’t bind you to a single model vendor. It’s an identity and contract layer; you decide which AI backends you support.

How ChatCard works

ChatCard is designed to feel simple for users and straightforward for developers, while staying flexible enough to support different AI stacks.

1. You create your card

On chatcard.cloud (or within a compatible app), you set up your profile:

  • Language(s) and preferred tone.
  • Accessibility preferences and content style.
  • Optional model preferences.

You get a secure, portable card—represented as a signed profile that lives in your control.

2. A site advertises ChatCard support

AI-enabled sites announce support for ChatCard via a standard header or meta tag. Your browser or companion app can then offer: “Use your ChatCard here?”

3. You grant scoped access

If you say yes, the site receives a scoped token with:

  • A pseudonymous identifier.
  • Your AI preferences relevant to that site.
  • The permissions you granted (e.g., read-only, suggest, act-with-confirmation).

4. Your AI goes to work

The site (or your own AI agent running client-side) uses this information to:

  • Render summaries and helpers in your style.
  • Interact with AI-enabled content on the page using exposed actions.
  • Respect your limits and privacy settings.

5. You stay in control

From your ChatCard dashboard, you can:

  • See which sites have access.
  • Adjust or revoke permissions.
  • Rotate keys or reset your card if needed.

Under the hood, ChatCard can use familiar identity technologies (JWT, OAuth/OIDC-style flows). On the surface, it’s just your AI showing up already on your side.

Trust, safety, and control

ChatCard is about giving people more control over how AI acts on their behalf—not less. The standard is being designed with a few non-negotiable principles:

  • You first. Your card exists to represent your preferences and limits, not to give unlimited power to apps or AIs.
  • Consent by design. Connections to new sites are explicit. Scopes are visible and revocable.
  • Provider-neutral. ChatCard doesn’t lock you into any one AI vendor or platform.
  • Clear contracts. Sites declare what they want AIs to do; your card declares what you allow. Anything beyond that is out of bounds.

As the spec matures, we expect to work with partners on independent audits, open documentation, and interoperable implementations.

Frequently asked questions

Is ChatCard a product, a standard, or both?

ChatCard starts as an open standard for portable AI identity, with a reference implementation at chatcard.cloud. Other apps and platforms can implement compatible cards if they follow the spec.

Does ChatCard replace my login?

No. ChatCard is about how AI recognizes and acts for you—not about account authentication. It can coexist with your existing login system.

Do I need to pick one AI provider?

No. ChatCard is provider-neutral. It can express preferences, but it doesn’t lock you or developers into a specific vendor.

How does this relate to EarthCloud and Funwae?

ChatCard is being designed in the same ecosystem as EarthCloud (intent & trails) and Funwae (atmosphere studio), but the goal is a standard that any stack can adopt.

Can my product or platform help shape the spec?

Yes. If you’re building AI-enabled content, browsers, or agent frameworks and want to collaborate on the ChatCard standard, please reach out.
